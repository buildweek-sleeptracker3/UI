Why Sleep Tracker?

What sets Sleep Tracker apart from the hundreds of other apps out there that record your sleep? Here are some key features that will help you see why Sleep Tracker is a cut above the rest.

Track Your Sleep

Sleep Tracker provides (as you would expect) the ability to enter in the hours you slept during the night. It may not be groundbreaking technology, but it is certainly the most important feature of the app!

You'll enter this information by selecting the date/time you went to sleep, and the date/time you woke up. The app will then calculate exactly how long you slept and store it in an entry for that day.

Rate Your Mood during the Day

When you wake up in the morning you'll be able to select one of 4 emojis displaying how you're feeling that day. These range from exhausted to feeling great! This will also get stored with your sleep session in the app.

This is where the magic starts to happen. Without these ratings, this is just a sleep log. But once the application has an idea of how you're feeling after sleeping a certain number of hours, you'll get access to an analysis of how much sleep you need.

View Your Sleep History

One page of the application allows you to view all of your previous sleep entries, which is nice if you'd like to check your sleep length trends or find out when you went to sleep on a given night.

From this page you are also able to edit and delete entries that may have been entered incorrectly. Your log will update immediately with the changes you've made to your sleep entries.

Get an Analysis - How Much Sleep do you Need?

Getting too much or too little sleep can be a hinderance on your productivity and energy levels the following day. Sleep Tracker aims to help you find the 30 minute window that has you feeling the best.

After you've entered enough sleep data, your entries will be analyzed and you'll get a 30 minute window of sleep to aim for. This is calculated by looking at your mood ratings and how much sleep you got the night before in order to determine your personal needs.
